This was a unique read. There was just enough mystery in the beginning to keep me hooked but not so much that I was left confused and reeling. It wasn't overtly science fiction until the story developed more, but even still it had an underlying flavor of science fiction that had me questioning the world. I loved the characters different motives as each one was a perspective I could relate to. Even the supposed antagonist was somebody that I felt I could be sucked into following along with. I think that's the sign of a good story arc, you're right there with them forming your own beliefs and opinions. I do want to read the sequel to this but maybe not immediately.
